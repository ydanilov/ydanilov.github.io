Project Goal

‍

Kinchaku is a Japanese-based b2b2c service that simplifies the design and distribution of mobile wallet-enabled point cards, stamp cards, discount coupons, membership cards, admission tickets, and event tickets.

 

Kinchaku helps small and medium-sized businesses to adopt native capabilities of Apple Wallet and Google Pay to deliver a flawless mobile experience.

My Role


I was responsible for product design and conducting user research. The product was fueled by my fast product iterations, optimized design workflow, and documentation. I worked closely with developers, the marketing team, engineers, and PM.

MEET THE USERS

Most of the time, products tend to usually just have one primary key path scenario. However, as a B2B2C business, Kinchaku has two key path scenarios.

 

Clients will experience as while planning an event or loyalty program, and their end-users will experience while browsing for better promotion or an event to visit.

Lo-fi Ideation

 

Working through a couple of really preliminary sketches, we realized some of the ideas and layouts that we were thinking of just wouldn't work. Not only were we able to quickly communicate some of the ideas we had a hard time explaining to each other, but we were also able to take them and spark more discussions as well as quickly filter the good and bad ideas out.

USER ANALYSiS

We conducted qualitative and quantitative researches.

Our surveys were aimed at measuring user needs and our compliance with them.

Using Google analytics we measured basic but so important indicators like crash reports and page load speed. But the most interesting option it gives is to trace the main routs of our users and try to understand what's the last task they did before they closed the app and did they realize their original goals.
