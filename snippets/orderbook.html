Simultaneously we developed our own approaches to the creation of the best user experience on top of that knowledge.

Design Culture Creation

Building the product is an iterative process requiring collaboration between multiple departments and our end users.

 

I have no doubts, that level of understanding of our users is a reflection of how well we interact with each other within the team. Building a company culture where the voices of all team members will be heard results in creating a product sensitive to users’ needs.

 

So to build the product that we all will be proud of, we need to set communication bridges between the design department and other departments of the company. It helps us to build a culture where each team member feels his influence on the product not only in his narrow specialization but in the service growth in general.

It doesn't make sense to hire smart people and then tell them what to do; we hire smart people so they can tell us what to do.

 

 — Steve Jobs

 

Who else but the development team knows all of the technical restrictions of the product? Nobody knows users’ attitudes better than the support team, and only quality engineers have enormous empirical evidence of working with our product in numerous environments. All of those aspects obviously have a direct impact on UX works.

I was lucky to work in a team of industrious and talented people. And I can’t imagine building a successful digital experience of our product without their input and vision in my work.

For instance, making inner meetups for presentations of raw prototypes for the team, was a great way to share findings and get everyone working on the product on the same page, evaluate ideas within the team, and make inner design processes much more transparent to the team.

 

Searching for empathy within the team helped us to create a synergy effect in our processes, which is extremely important in startups like ours.

Submersion & Idea Generation

Holding Needfinding sessions and interviews with key stakeholders we determined our business needs and target audience.

 

I did competitors’ research, to find out the best practice and market standards.

 

Based on initial the data got from the research and our assumptions I created several proto-personas to reflect our user's portraits.

 

Then I built the Value Proposition Canvas, to ensure that our vision of the product is positioned right with customers’ values and needs.

 

UX Engineering

Based on Value Proposition Canvases I designed User Scenarios (in fact, basic UX Flow Schemes), which will lead users to achieve their goals in the shortest way.

 

As the first step of building wireframes, I produced Information Architecture schemes organizing and structuring the content of websites

 

I created lo-fi wireframes and gathered them into User Flow Schemes to get a flow that both users and developers will understand.

 

All the above helped me to approach UX from a holistic point of view.

 

Design and validation

I created hi-fi UI mockups and clickable prototypes when needed.

 

Collectively with the product managers, I created User Stories in an Agile manner for the development team.

 

I supervised continuous integration processes with the development team and provided an implementation accuracy check before the feature release.

 

UX Analisis

I gathered and analyzed statistics and user feedback, checked assumptions we have made in the beginning.

Based on all the collected information (personas, timeline, emotion, touchpoints, channels) I created User Journey Maps for each user scenario.

 

It helped us find the product’s weaknesses and consequently improve the user’s satisfaction and raising the chances of a digital product’s success overall.

Product & Services

Orderbook serve the interests of two groups of users: traders and investors.

They were looking for different gains, so we developed a number of services within our ecosystem to meet their needs.

 

General Orderbook.io UX scheme

Token Exchange

The service was intended for crypto traders, with the option to trade between popular cryptocurrencies and listed token, and vice versa.

Token sale dashboard

Also, it had some tools for observing trends using typical exchange market approaches. In each case, we placed emphasis both on trading information, and the project behind the token since the field of the project activity and project newsfeed has a direct impact on asset exchange rates.

We visualized the maximum of useful information about smart contracts operation on UI to let a user understand deeper background processes behind trading.

 

Widespread Implementation of Etherscan links helped us to use Blockchain component for increasing transparency and confidence.

Token Sale wizard

The service was intended for contributors as end-users. By itself, the service was b2b Ethereum based software, which allows our customers to launch legally compliant token sales in a secure and reliable way.

Wizard was an easy-to-integrate tool that covered all the aspects required to conduct an ICO: registration, KYC/AML, and Accredited Investor Status verifications, deposit, and contribution all within one intuitive flow. Any user previously registered at Orderbook is able to do a one-click purchase.

 

Just after the launch of the first instance of our product, we started working on developing Wizard builder to cover all kinds of clients’ needs in all stages of a token sale.

 

One of the goals was to produce a flexible tool with an advanced scope of settings from technical ones as in regional permissions, to marketing as in bonus periods and sales. Another goal was to cover it with a number of customizable skins to choose.

 

Crypto wallet

It was intended equally for all categories of our users. We had no doubts that in the near future we will see deeper integration of cryptocurrencies and tokenized assets in our everyday life. Anyone who becomes interested in cryptocurrency eventually needs to set up a private wallet to store and manage their assets.

 

Designing a wallet we treat it as a widely spread tool in the long term. That’s why high standards of usability were an important aspect for us.

 

After a deep competitor analysis, we decided to make it a single-page solution, simplifying access to any function in the wallet.


Multicurrency crypto portfolio

For instance, the history section consists of all the information about any movements of user funds in the Orderbook ecosystem deposit/withdraws, buy/sell, or initial token sale participation.

 

For trading operations there, it also had two views: chronological and grouped by order (for orders which were matched in several takes).

 

One of the most challenging things, which was equally important for all our services, was producing a simple flow for the KYC form which consists of a huge number of different kinds of input fields.

 

Being 100% compliant we had to ask users for a variety of personal information in a number of fields. An ambitious goal for me was to minimize pain along that way.

 

Manipulating with smart UI decisions, grouping and rearranging components between steps I achieved increasing of average form passing speed by up to 30%, and reduced the number of incomplete attends to pass verification.

Design System

Maintaining consistency and scalability in big products is impossible to achieve without a system comprising styles, components, UX guidelines, and marketing patterns across multiple products in one ecosystem.

 

The main goal was to give production teams a consistent design language and pattern library to solve common visual, UX, and technical challenges.

 

So we provided a Design System as a platform for radical collaboration between design, development, marketing, and QA departments. It consisted of:

Sketch templates

UX guidelines

HTML/CSS/ReactJS components

Organized symbols library, based on atomic design principles.

Sets of recommendations to provide clear instructions to designers and developers on how to adopt specific UX principles

Living systems with actual code components at their core.

 

Design System is not just a set of elements, but information about how to use them together, but the way to show the product patterns and the concepts behind it.

 

It's a way to organize our knowledge about design components and technical aspects of development.

 

Building common concepts and terminology within the team, to collaborate better we create a cohesive components map at the first stage.

 

 

 

Frequently designers give developers a set of #HEX colors codes, which they will add later to the code as variables. In this case, designers transmit colors, with no dependencies between them and no logic of its usage.

 

Although using math dependencies in colors is what a modern designer does at the latest stages of building a template. So instead of providing final #HEX codes, I created library guidelines with all the sets of variables used for colors and typography, and sass mixins ready for implementation in React-based components.
